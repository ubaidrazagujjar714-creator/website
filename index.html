TO MY DEAREST MAM MUQADAS

From Saba Akram - BS IT 2021-2025

As I stand at the threshold of graduation, my heart is filled with emotions that words can hardly express. This journey that began four years ago has been transformative, and at the center of this transformation stands you - my guide, my mentor, my inspiration.

I remember my first class with you - nervous, unsure, and overwhelmed by the complexity of programming. But with your first smile and those encouraging words, "Don't worry, we'll learn this together," you turned my anxiety into anticipation. You didn't just teach code; you taught us how to think like programmers, how to solve problems, how to be resilient when things didn't work, and how to celebrate when they did.

Your classroom was never just a physical space; it was a sanctuary of learning where mistakes were welcomed as opportunities to grow, where questions were encouraged as signs of curiosity, and where every student felt seen and valued. You had this incredible ability to look at a confused face and know exactly what part needed clarification.

What I admire most about you is not just your vast knowledge of information technology, but your profound understanding of human potential. You saw in me capabilities I didn't know I possessed. You pushed me when I needed pushing, supported me when I needed support, and believed in me even when I doubted myself.

The lessons you taught me extend far beyond textbooks and syllabi. You taught me perseverance when debugging seemed impossible. You taught me attention to detail when writing requirements. You taught me teamwork during project collaborations. You taught me professionalism that will guide my career. These are not just academic lessons; these are life lessons.

As I receive my degree, I know this achievement is as much yours as it is mine. Every line of code I write, every problem I solve, every project I complete - they all bear the imprint of your teachings. You have given me more than an education; you have given me a foundation for life.

This website is a small token of my immense gratitude. While digital code can never fully capture the depth of my appreciation, I hope it serves as a reminder that your impact extends far beyond classroom walls. You have shaped not just a student, but a future professional, and more importantly, a better human being.

Thank you for being the teacher every student dreams of having. Thank you for your patience, your wisdom, your kindness, and your unwavering belief in your students. Thank you for being my guiding star.

As I step into the world, I carry your teachings with me. I will make you proud. I will apply everything you taught me. And whenever I achieve something meaningful, I will remember that it began in your classroom.

Saba Akram
BS Information Technology | Session 2021-2025 | Graduation 2025
